Learn English with Confidence

Welcome to your English learning page. Here you will find grammar tips, vocabulary practice, and useful examples all in English.

Grammar

Grammar is the foundation of clear English. Practice the simple present, past, future, and conditional sentences every day.

  • Present Simple: I study English every day.
  • Past Simple: She visited the museum yesterday.
  • Future Simple: They will finish the project tomorrow.
  • Conditional: If I have time, I will read a book.

Grammar Formulas

Use these formula patterns for positive, negative, and question sentences.

Present Simple

Positive: Subject + base verb (+ s/es for he/she/it)

Negative: Subject + do/does not + base verb

Question: Do/Does + subject + base verb?

Example: She does not like coffee. Does she like coffee?

Past Simple

Positive: Subject + verb + ed / second form

Negative: Subject + did not + base verb

Question: Did + subject + base verb?

Example: We did not visit the museum. Did you visit the museum?

Future Simple

Positive: Subject + will + base verb

Negative: Subject + will not + base verb

Question: Will + subject + base verb?

Example: I will study later. Will you study later?

Present Continuous

Positive: Subject + am/is/are + verb + ing

Negative: Subject + am/is/are not + verb + ing

Question: Am/Is/Are + subject + verb + ing?

Example: She is reading. Is she reading?

Vocabulary

Learn new words with meaning and use them in sentences. Try to review them every day.

Study

To learn or review information carefully. Example: I study English grammar.

Practice

To do something again and again to improve. Example: She practices pronunciation daily.

Conversation

Talking with another person in English. Example: We have a short conversation in class.

Vocabulary Formulas

Use these patterns to build new words and sentences correctly.

Noun + Verb

Subject + verb + object. Example: I read a book.

Adjective + Noun

Use an adjective before a noun. Example: She has a big house.

Verb + to + Infinitive

Use after verbs like want, need, hope, help. Example: I want to learn English.

Adverb Form

Add -ly to adjectives. Example: quick → quickly, easy → easily.

Plural Nouns

Add -s or -es. Example: cat → cats, box → boxes.

Comparative / Superlative

Use -er / -est or more / most. Example: tall → taller → tallest.

Reading & Listening

Reading and listening help you understand English faster. Start with simple stories, songs, or videos.

Example: Listen to a short audio and read the same sentence. Then repeat the words aloud.

Practice Activities

  1. Write one sentence about your day in English.
  2. Find five new English words and use them in a sentence.
  3. Read a short text and identify verbs and nouns.
